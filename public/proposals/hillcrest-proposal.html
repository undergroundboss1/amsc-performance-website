Section 01

Executive Summary

AMSC Performance proposes a co-branded Youth Athletic Development & Wellbeing Holiday Camp for Hillcrest International Schools — a five-day program that combines professional-grade movement training with age-appropriate performance testing and wellbeing education.

The camp is designed for students aged 10–12 and will serve a maximum of 20 participants. Each session runs 2–3 hours and focuses on developing foundational athletic qualities: movement literacy, speed, coordination, strength, and body awareness.

Every student will undergo baseline performance profiling using Dashr timing gates and jump mat assessments, receiving an individual digital report that tracks their development against age-appropriate reference bands.

Beyond physical training, the program integrates practical wellbeing education — covering sleep, hydration, nutrition basics, and recovery — equipping students with habits that support both athletic and academic performance.

For Hillcrest, this program positions the school at the forefront of holistic student development, offering a differentiated co-curricular experience that parents and students value — with measurable outcomes and professional delivery.

Section 02

The Problem

Why structured youth athletic development matters now more than ever

Globally, youth physical activity levels are declining — and Kenya is not immune. Without structured intervention, young people face compounding risks to their health, development, and long-term athletic potential.

The Current Landscape

  • ▼
    81% of adolescents worldwide do not meet the WHO-recommended 60 minutes of daily physical activity (WHO, 2022)
  • ▼
    Youth sport injuries have risen by approximately 50% over the past two decades, driven largely by early specialization and poor movement foundations (Brenner, 2007; DiFiori et al., 2014)
  • ▼
    Sedentary behavior during school holidays increases screen time by an average of 2+ hours per day (Brazendale et al., 2017)
  • ▼
    Fewer than 25% of children aged 6–17 in sub-Saharan Africa participate in organized physical activity programs (Guthold et al., 2020)

The Consequences

  • →
    Poor movement literacy — children lack basic coordination, balance, and body awareness needed for safe sport participation
  • →
    Increased injury risk — without foundational strength and movement quality, the likelihood of preventable injuries rises significantly
  • →
    Declining confidence — physically inactive youth report lower self-efficacy and are less likely to engage in sport long-term
  • →
    Missed developmental windows — ages 10–12 represent a critical period for neuromuscular and motor skill development
Global Youth Physical Inactivity Rates (Insufficient Activity)
Source: WHO Global Status Report on Physical Activity, 2022 · Guthold et al., The Lancet Child & Adolescent Health, 2020

The window of opportunity is narrow. Research consistently shows that movement skills not developed between ages 6–12 become significantly harder to acquire later. A structured program during this period can set the foundation for a lifetime of healthy, active participation in sport.

Program Overview

What we deliver — and how it works

The Youth Athletic Development & Wellbeing Camp is a five-day progressive program that develops foundational athletic qualities through structured, coach-led sessions. Each day follows a deliberate training flow — warm-up, skill development, training block, wellbeing module, and cool-down.

Program Design Principles

Progressive Overload

Each session builds on the last — complexity and intensity increase gradually across the five days, respecting developmental readiness.

Movement Quality First

Technique and control are prioritized over speed or load. Athletes learn to move well before they move fast.

Inclusive by Design

All activities are scaled to individual ability. The program welcomes all fitness levels — not just "sporty" kids.

Data-Informed

Baseline and post-camp testing provide objective feedback, giving every student a personal development picture.

Professional-grade assessment tools for youth athletes

Every participant undergoes standardized performance profiling at the start and end of the camp. This provides objective, measurable feedback on physical development — motivating athletes and giving parents and the school clear evidence of progress.

⏱

Laser-precision electronic timing system used by professional sports teams worldwide.

10m Sprint — Measures acceleration and starting power

20m Sprint — Measures speed development and running mechanics

↑

Jump Mat Assessment

Contact-based jump measurement platform for assessing lower-body power and neuromuscular function.

Countermovement Jump — Measures vertical power output

Flight Time — Indicator of explosive strength development

Deliverables & Reporting

Transparent outcomes for parents and school leadership

Data is only valuable when it's communicated clearly. Every stakeholder receives structured, professional reporting that demonstrates the value of the program and the development of each student.

For Parents
Individual Digital Performance Report
  • ◆
    Baseline vs. post-camp results for all tested metrics (10m, 20m sprint times, vertical jump height)
  • ◆
    Age-appropriate development bands — showing where the child sits relative to expected ranges (not peer rankings)
  • ◆
    Movement quality observations — coach notes on strengths and areas for development
  • ◆
    Wellbeing habits summary — key takeaways and practical recommendations for home
  • ◆
    Personalized next steps — simple activities to continue development after camp
For the School
Aggregated Performance Summary
  • ◆
    Group performance overview — anonymized aggregate data showing cohort trends and averages
  • ◆
    Key findings and patterns — notable observations across the group (e.g., common movement limitations)
  • ◆
    Improvement metrics — percentage of students who improved across each tested domain
  • ◆
    Wellbeing insights — common knowledge gaps and recommendations for school PE programming
  • ◆
    Recommendations for future programming — data-informed suggestions for the next camp or term-time integration

Important Note on Data Privacy: All individual student data is confidential and shared only with the student's parent/guardian. The school receives only aggregated, anonymized data. No individual results are shared with peers, teachers, or other parents. AMSC Performance adheres to responsible data handling practices appropriate for working with minors.

Understanding Development Bands

Reports use development bands rather than rankings or percentiles. This approach is intentional — it focuses on where each child is in their own development journey, not how they compare to others. Bands are informed by age-appropriate reference data and categorized as:

Developing
Building foundational capacity
Competent
Meeting age-appropriate benchmarks
Proficient
Exceeding expected development
Advanced
High athletic development potential
